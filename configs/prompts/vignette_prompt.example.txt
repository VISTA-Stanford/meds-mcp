# Clinical Vignette Generation Prompt Template (USMLE-style narrative)
# Copy this file to vignette_prompt.txt and customize as needed.
# The .txt file is gitignored; this .example.txt is tracked.

You are generating a clinical vignette for patient similarity retrieval in thoracic oncology. Rewrite the following patient timeline into a USMLE-style clinical vignette — a single coherent narrative paragraph written in clinical prose, not structured sections.

Style example (content must come entirely from the user's timeline; this is shown only to illustrate the format):
"A 68-year-old man with a 40-pack-year smoking history and hypertension was diagnosed with stage IIIB lung adenocarcinoma in 03/2021 after a right upper lobe mass was identified on CT. Molecular profiling showed an EGFR exon 19 deletion. He received concurrent chemoradiation with cisplatin and etoposide from 04/2021 to 07/2021, followed by consolidation durvalumab. Restaging CT in 01/2022 demonstrated stable disease. Current medications include lisinopril and osimertinib. He is a former smoker and reports ECOG performance status 1."

Rules:
- One paragraph of clinical prose, third person, past tense for historical events and present tense for the current state.
- Open with demographics (age, sex) and the most relevant past medical / social history (smoking history, prior malignancies, major comorbidities) that anchors the case.
- Weave diagnosis (with histology and stage if stated), molecular markers, treatment course (drug/regimen names with approximate MM/YYYY dates), disease-status updates, imaging/pathology milestones, and comorbidities into the narrative in roughly chronological order.
- Include specific values only when they are explicitly reported AND clinically material (e.g., PD-L1 TPS, Ki67, TNM stage, ECOG, abnormal labs that changed management). Omit routine lab tables and administrative events.
- Use standard oncology abbreviations (NSCLC, SCLC, adeno, squam, mets, CR/PR/SD/PD, RUL/RML/RLL/LUL/LLL).
- Preserve only explicitly stated information. Never infer, speculate, diagnose, or assign causality beyond what the timeline states.
- If a domain (e.g., molecular markers, comorbidities) is absent from the timeline, simply omit it — do not write placeholders like "none documented".
- Target length: 4–8 sentences.
- Output only the vignette itself. No preamble, headers, bullet points, or trailing commentary.
